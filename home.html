How can we redesign a public library website?

https://www.southboroughlib.org/

Above is the current website for the Southborough Public Library, a local library not too far from where I live. We can quickly see that it's on the older side with plenty of scope for improvement. Interested in redesigning it, I first explored problems with usability and accessibility.

Usability Issues

  • Searching the catalog, which is one of the most common reasons for vising a library page, is not immediately accessible from the home page. The user has to first click on "Browse our Catalog"
  • The text blocks and links feel very crowded. Clicking through a series of small links is not very memorizable
  • The images buttons on the right-hand side are old and grainy
  • There are too many distractions and links throughout the page in addition to the main top menu bar. It does not feel cohesive
  • The row of books at the bottom of the screen also seems packed and distracting

Accessibility

  • There are several empty links and areas with low contrast between the text and background colors
  • The screen reader reads through countless small icons which give minimal context to the user, but it is good that all the icons have alternative text
  • The center section is a slideshow, with each slide having a lot of text. The page could change the current slide before the screen reader has finished reading it
  • There are a few redundant links

The new design successfully responds to different computer screens and mobile devices. It uses a fewer number of and higher resolution images, and as a result the screen reader needs to go through much less alternative text.

Compared to the original website, the user needs to go through much less visual and accessible input in order to understand the page. Most importantly, they can immediately find common information they are looking for, such as when the library is open, if a certain book or online resource is available, or if they want to reach out to the library directly.
